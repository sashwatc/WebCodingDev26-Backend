# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pleasant Valley High School students, staff finders, main-office administrators, and FBLA judges use this platform. Students need to report missing items quickly, browse redacted found items, and submit ownership evidence. Staff and admins need a reliable workflow for intake, claim review, audit trails, and pickup communication.

## Product Purpose

Lost Then Found helps a school recover misplaced items with a safer workflow than a public list or basic CRUD table. Success means a student can report a lost item, see possible matches, submit a private claim, and have an admin verify the claim with a clear record of what changed.

## Brand Personality

Operational, trustworthy, student-centered. The experience should feel like a real school service: calm enough for repeated admin use, polished enough for NLC judging, and clear enough for a student to finish a task without help.

## Anti-references

This should not look like a single-page CRUD demo, a marketing landing page, a decorative dashboard, or an unsecured public inventory that exposes owner-verification details. Avoid hidden div navigation, unclear form errors, toy admin controls, and UI that depends on one desktop viewport.

## Design Principles

- Make the recovery workflow visible: lost report, possible match, claim, admin review, pickup notification.
- Protect private evidence while still giving students enough public detail to recognize a likely item.
- Prefer explainable scoring and human review over fake AI certainty.
- Keep admin workflows dense, scannable, and auditable.
- Design for school devices first: phone, tablet, Chromebook, keyboard, and high-contrast browser settings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA fundamentals: semantic routes, keyboard-friendly forms, visible focus states, high contrast text, accessible labels, helpful errors, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts for phone, tablet, and desktop.
